steps:
  - "Gather [[water|quantity=600]] and a large mixing bowl."
  - "Pour [[water|quantity=600]] into the mixing bowl and add [[salt|quantity=30]]. Mix well with your hand until the [[salt]] dissolves."
  - "Add 100 g (10% of the flour) to the [[water]] and mix with your hand until the flour dissolves — this will create a thin crepe/pancake-like consistency."
  - "Add 1–[[fresh-yeast|quantity=2]] (about 1.5 g in this recipe) to the [[water]] and mix with your fingers until it dissolves."
  - "Now add the remaining Le 5 Stagioni Tipo 00 flour (make up to 1 kg total) a small handful at a time using one hand to add flour and the other hand to mix; do not add all the flour at once."
  - "Continue mixing with your hands until the dough starts to come together."
  - "Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured bench and knead with both hands, absorbing any remaining flour, until the dough is smooth and cohesive."
  - "Keep working the dough until it has a smooth surface. To test if it's worked enough, press one finger into the dough ball — if it bounces back, it is likely ready."
  - "Alternatively, check the dough temperature by inserting a thermometer into the core — aim for 23°C–26°C (73.4°F–74.8°F)."
  - "Place the dough aside on a flat surface, cover with a damp cloth so it doesn't dry out, and leave to rest for at least 2 hours."
  - "After 2 hours, divide the dough and weigh each portion aiming for 250 g (≈8.8 oz) per dough ball."
  - "To form a dough ball, roll the piece in a circular motion in the same spot using the palm of your hand until you have a smooth, round surface."
  - "Place the formed dough balls in a flat, airtight container and leave at ambient temperature between 16°C–18°C (60.8°F–64.4°F) for 24 hours for cold/slow fermentation."
  - "After 24 hours the dough balls should have risen and be ready to use. Sprinkle flour onto the bench and place a dough ball on top."
  - "Start shaping from about an inch from the bottom and pressing with your fingers, then stop and turn the dough over and repeat until you have a small round base with a raised cornicione (crust)."
  - "Gently stretch the base by draping it over one forearm and slightly stretching, then flip onto the bench; repeat as needed to form a circle and rest the base on the bench before topping."
  - "HOW TO MAKE A NEAPOLITAN MARGHERITA:"
  - "Spread [[italian-tomato-sauce]] onto the stretched base using the back of a spoon in a circular motion."
  - "Add [[fresh-basil-leaves]], a sprinkle of [[dry-oregano]], a drizzle of [[extra-virgin-olive-oil]] and thinly sliced [[cherry-tomatoes]] to taste."
  - "Place the pizza in a wood-fired oven at around 400°C (or use the maximum temperature on your home oven with a pizza stone) and cook quickly until done."
  - "To cook evenly, pull the pizza out with a peel, turn it, then push it back in so both sides cook."
  - "When ready, break up a large [[buffalo-mozzarella]] with your hands and scatter the pieces over the cooked pizza just before serving."
  - "HOW TO MAKE NEAPOLITAN PIZZA DOUGH WITH A STAND MIXER:"
  - "Add all ingredients to the stand mixer but do not add all the flour at once; mix so the dough absorbs most of the flour."
  - "When the dough has absorbed most flour, turn it out onto the bench and finish kneading by hand until smooth."
  - "Easiest way to finish a dough ball: roll it in a circular motion in one spot with the palm of your hand until the surface is smooth and round."
  - "Top tips: never let [[salt]] contact the [[fresh-yeast]] directly when combining ingredients; always add the 10% flour to the [[water]] first to protect the [[fresh-yeast]]."
  - "If you need to freeze dough: freeze after you have formed and rested the dough balls (after the first 2‑hour rest) in airtight containers or bags; thaw in the fridge overnight then leave at room temperature for 3–4 hours before stretching."
notes:
  - "Do not mix salt directly with the yeast — add 10% flour to the water first to avoid contaminating the yeast."
  - "Aim for a dough temperature of 23°C–26°C for best fermentation control; use cooler water or shorter kneading if your dough gets too warm."
  - "If you don't have a wood-fired oven, use your oven's maximum temperature and a pizza stone for best results."
  - "To freeze: freeze dough balls after they’ve relaxed following the initial 2-hour rest; thaw in the fridge overnight and then warm to room temperature for 3–4 hours before using."
