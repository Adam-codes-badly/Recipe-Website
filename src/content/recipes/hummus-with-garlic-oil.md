steps:
  - "Put [[dried-chickpeas]] in a large bowl and cover with cold water to twice their depth. Leave to soak for at least 14 hours, no more than 24 hours, changing the water halfway through soaking."
  - "Drain and rinse the soaked [[dried-chickpeas]], then place them in a large saucepan. Cover with fresh cold water and bring to the boil. Boil for 10 minutes, then reduce to a steady simmer."
  - "Carefully add [[bicarbonate-of-soda]] to the simmering [[dried-chickpeas]]. Continue to simmer until the [[dried-chickpeas]] are very soft when smashed with your fingers. Start checking after about 1 to 1 1/4 hours; cooking can take up to 2 hours depending on the [[dried-chickpeas|display=batch of chickpeas]]."
  - "When cooked, drain the [[dried-chickpeas|quantity=1000|display=cooked chickpeas]] well. You should have approximately [[dried-chickpeas|quantity=1000|display=cooked, drained chickpeas]]. If you prefer some texture in the hummus, chill the [[dried-chickpeas|quantity=1000|display=cooked chickpeas]] before blending; blend while hot for an extra silky result."
  - "To make the garlic oil, put [[garlic-cloves-peeled]] in a small saucepan and cover with [[olive-oil]]. Bring to a low simmer, then turn the heat down and cook for about 5 minutes until the garlic is soft and a little browned. Drain the garlic from the [[olive-oil|display=garlic oil]] and reserve the [[olive-oil|display=garlic oil]] and the softened garlic separately."
  - "Put the cooked [[dried-chickpeas|quantity=1000|display=chickpeas]], 4 of the softened garlic cloves, [[ground-cumin]], [[salt]], and [[lemon-juice]] into a food processor and start to blend."
  - "With the processor running, add [[tahini]]; the mixture will thicken. Immediately add [[iced-water]] a little at a time until you reach the desired consistency. Blend to your preferred smoothness."
  - "Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve the hummus in a bowl with a drizzle of the reserved [[olive-oil|display=garlic oil]] and the remaining 2 softened garlic cloves on top."
notes:
  - "Soaking: leave chickpeas at least 14 hours but no more than 24 to avoid sprouting; change the soaking water halfway."
  - "Bicarbonate of soda helps the chickpeas cook evenly and soften more quickly; add carefully as it froths on contact."
  - "Texture: blend while the chickpeas are hot for very smooth hummus; chill them first if you prefer some texture."
  - "Iced water helps loosen and cool the mix for a lighter texture; add gradually until you reach the consistency you like."
  - "Storage: keeps in the fridge for up to 4 to 5 days. Hummus will thicken in the fridge, so loosen with a little water or olive oil before serving."
