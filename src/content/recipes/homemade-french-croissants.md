steps:
  - "Dissolve 5 g honey in 85 g lukewarm [[milk]] and whisk in [[active-dry-yeast|quantity=6]]; leave 10–20 minutes until foamy (activated [[active-dry-yeast]])."
  - "Add 60 g lukewarm [[water]], 25 g melted and cooled butter, [[white-sugar|quantity=20]], [[plain-all-purpose-flour|quantity=250]] and [[salt|quantity=5]] to the bowl and stir to form a shaggy détrempe (dough)."
  - "Knead the dough by hand for 1–2 minutes, then on a lightly floured surface for about 4 minutes until smooth; return to a bowl, cover and proof in a warm place until doubled in size (about 1 hour)."
  - "Turn out the proofed détrempe, press gently to remove air, place on a folded 14 x 10 in parchment and shape to a 7 x 10.5 in (18 x 27 cm) rectangle; wrap in the parchment case, seal and freeze for a few hours or overnight so the dough is cold but still pliable."
  - "While the dough proofs or chills, make the butter block (tourage): slice 140 g cold unsalted butter and arrange within a 5 x 6.5 in marked rectangle on parchment, fold the parchment to enclose the butter and use a rolling pin to hit and roll the block until pliable and even; chill in the fridge until firm."
  - "When ready to laminate, remove the frozen détrempe and let it thaw until cold but pliable (around 4–10°C); remove the butter block and make it pliable with a rolling pin while keeping it cold."
  - "Unwrap the détrempe and roll it lightly so it is an even 7 x 10.5 in rectangle; place the butter block (tourage) (still on parchment) onto one half of the dough, pat to bind, peel off the top parchment and fold the dough over to fully enclose the butter, sealing the seams."
  - "First lamination (double fold): on a lightly floured surface and keeping the short side as the width, roll the dough to about 40 cm length while maintaining an even width (~13 cm), trim edges, fold about 1/8th in from one short edge, bring the other short edge to meet it, then fold the whole piece in half (book fold) to create the double fold; make small corner cuts to release tension, wrap and chill 30–60 minutes."
  - "Second lamination (single fold): with the short side as the width, roll to ~38 cm length keeping the width at ~12–13 cm, brush off excess flour, fold the dough in thirds (single fold), cut folded corners to release tension, wrap and chill another 30–60 minutes."
  - "Sheeting stage 1: place the dough on a well-floured surface with the folded edge facing you, roll out to about 1 cm thickness and roughly 8–9 in width, then wrap and refrigerate for 30–45 minutes to relax the gluten."
  - "Sheeting stage 2: roll the rested dough to 4–5 mm thickness and to a width of about 9.5–10 in (23–24 cm); trim a thin strip from both long edges so the final width is ~23 cm and the edges are straight."
  - "Mark and cut triangles: along one long edge mark intervals of 9 cm and on the opposite edge mark midpoints between those marks; connect marks with straight lines and cut with a pizza cutter or sharp knife to yield about 6 triangles."
  - "Shape croissants: brush off excess flour from each triangle, make a 1 cm cut at the midpoint of the base, roll from the base while gently stretching the two base corners to widen it, continue rolling to the tip and tuck the tip under the croissant so it won't unravel; place each rolled croissant on a lined half-sheet tray (6 per tray)."
  - "Proof the shaped croissants: loosely cover with plastic wrap (optionally place another tray on top to create a warm enclosure) and proof at ≈25°C until doubled and wobbling slightly when the tray is shaken (about 2–4 hours depending on temperature)."
  - "Make the egg wash by whisking 1 [[egg-yolk]] with [[milk|quantity=2]] or cream until smooth; very gently brush the proofed croissants with the egg wash using a soft brush, taking care not to deflate them."
  - "Preheat the oven for at least 30 minutes to 190°C (conventional). Bake the croissants on the middle rack at 190°C for 20–30 minutes until puffed and deep golden brown, turning the tray once halfway if needed."
  - "Remove croissants from the oven and let them rest for a few minutes on the tray, then transfer to a wire rack to cool further so the interior structure can set before slicing."
notes:
  - "If layers are missing the butter either melted into the dough (environment too warm) or the butter broke during lamination (butter too cold/brittle). Keep dough and butter cold but pliable."
  - "If croissant dough keeps shrinking while rolling, wrap and chill for at least 30 minutes to relax the gluten before continuing."
  - "If the butter is very low in fat, mix the butter with 10% flour (by weight) and work it until smooth before forming the tourrage to reduce brittleness."
  - "Leftover trim pieces can be rolled into an extra croissant, filled to make pain au chocolat, or frozen for later."
  - "Preferred timeline: spread over 2–3 days — day 1 make dough and freeze, day 2 laminate and rest, day 3 cut, proof and bake — but experienced bakers can compress stages if needed."
