steps:
  - "Preheat the oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7."
  - "Sit [[lamb-shoulder-skin-on]] skin-side up on a board. Using a sharp knife, make irregular holes through the skin and push [[garlic-cloves-sliced]] into the holes."
  - "Place the lamb shoulder in a roasting tin and roast for about 20 minutes until browned. Carefully turn the lamb and roast for a further 20-30 minutes until brown all over."
  - "Meanwhile, boil [[large-onions-quartered]] in a pan of water for 10 minutes. Add [[waxy-potatoes-peeled-and-sliced-2cm-thick]] and boil both for 4 minutes, then drain well."
  - "Remove the lamb shoulder from the tin. Add the par-boiled onions and potatoes to the tin and mix together. Pour in [[hot-lamb-or-beef-stock]], put the lamb back on top and cover the tin tightly with kitchen foil."
  - "Reduce the oven temperature to 160C/140C fan/gas 3 and cook the covered lamb shoulder for 3 1/2 to 4 hours, or until the lamb is completely tender."
  - "When the lamb is cooked, remove it from the tin and transfer to a board. Strain off the fat from the tin into a small bowl for the gravy. Increase the oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7 and return the potatoes and onions to the oven to brown for 25 minutes."
  - "To make the gravy, spoon [[lamb-fat-for-gravy]] into a saucepan. Add [[plain-flour]] and stir over the heat for about 30 seconds. Gradually whisk in [[hot-lamb-or-beef-stock-for-gravy]] and [[red-wine]] until smooth, then stir in [[redcurrant-jelly]], [[worcestershire-sauce-a-few-drops|mode=amountOnly]] of Worcestershire sauce and gravy browning if using. Whisk until thickened and bubbling."
  - "Carve the lamb shoulder and serve with the red-wine gravy, the browned potatoes and onions, and [[green-vegetables-to-serve]]."
notes:
  - "Use waxy potatoes so they hold their shape during the long cook."
  - "If your lamb needs longer to become tender, leave it in the oven; slow roasting makes it forgiving and very soft."
